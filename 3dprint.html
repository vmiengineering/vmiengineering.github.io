Frequently Asked Questions

  1. FILE FORMAT
    All files must be a stereolithography file, with an STL extension (.STL)
  2. PRINT VOLUME DIMENSIONS
    MakerGear M2:: 190 [X-AXIS] X 190MM [Y-AXIS] X 240MM [Z-AXIS]
    Stratasys F170::
    Ultimaker:: 
  3. FILAMENT TYPE
    MakerGear m2 printer uses PLA filament only.  We will not use user filament.
    Stratasys
    Ultimaker
  4. RESOLUTION,  INFILL, & PRINT SPEEDS
    To help faciliate efficiency in time and aim towards low waste of materials all prints will be considered a rapid prototype and settings will be determined by the Lab Tech's discretion unless otherwise stated.  If it is a structural print or a final print please let the Lab Tech's know of requested infill and resolution change.  If you are not familiar with what settings you need speak with a lab tech.
  5. MULTIPLE PARTS
    If your model has multiple parts please seperate them all into their own .STL file.
  6. HOW LONG WILL MY PRINT TAKE?
    There are many factors that determine how long the print can take and is no definitive answer.  To read about factors that can effect the time please see resouces here:
    3Dprinterly.com Article
    3Space.com Article with picture examples

    To give a rough example (again all dependent on model, speed, infill, resolution, layer height, skirt, raft, brim, wall thickness etc) one lego may take approximately 4 minutes, a cell phone case - 20 minutes, baseball with 15% infill - 2 hours, tesla coil holder - 6-12 hours, transmission case (15 x 10 x 5) - multiple days.  Previous years ECE robot bumpers could take upwards to 14 hours depending.  Again this is rough estimates.

    Other considerations to account for are failed prints.  We have had it before where a print can go perfectly for 10 hours and have layer separation right at the end.  Additionally, improper model designs can greatly effect printing such as too tight of tolerances, walls not being thick enough, empty walls etc.

    Also, if there are other prints waiting to be printed before yours it can take up to a week or two before yours will be printed.
  7. PRINTING RESTRICTIONS 
    We reserve the right to refuse any requests that are: 
  8. - Complicated prints with very tight tolerances
    - Prohibitied by local, state, or federal law
    - Unsafe, dangerous, or pose a threat to the well being of others
    - Offensive or inappropriate content  
    - Intended for resale
